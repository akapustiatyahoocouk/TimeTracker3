Works and Events

TimeTracker3 uses Works and Events to record the daily log of what each team member was working on. This allows both managing one's daily effort on a day-by-day basis and integrating this daily logs into aggregated activity/effort reports.

Works

A Work (also called a Work Unit) is a record of the fact that a specific period of time was dedicated to working on a specific Activity or Task. For such a Work Unit, the following information is recorded:

  • Its start and end times.
  • Its associated Account (who did the work?)
  • Its associated Activity or Task (what was the time spent on?)

TimeTracker3 will normally not allow removing recorded Work Units from the historical data. Of course, someone acting as a "workspace administrator" may do anything they need within the managed workspace, including the removal or old or irrelevant Work Units but even then the practice is strongly discouraged.

Events

An Event is a record that, at some moment in the past, something relevant to the daily work has happened. There are several kinds of Events that TimeTracker3 recognises and records:

  • Activity (or Task) start/end events - TimeTracker3 allows configuring specific Activities (and Tasks) to actively require the user to enter a comment when starting or stopping that specific Activity or Task (or both when starting and stopping the one).
  • Task completion comments - TimeTracker3 allows configuring Tasks so as to actively require the user to enter a comment when marking that Task as "completed".
  • Arbitrary comments - TimeTracker allows the user to enter an arbitrary comment at any time during the working day, even if that user is currently working on some Activity or Task. These comments allow for better historical perspective on what happened during a given day.

For every Event, the following information is recorded:

  • Its occurrence time.
  • Its associated Account (who logged the comment?)
  • Its associated Activities or Tasks (which of these the comment is related to?)

Similarly to recorded Work Units, TimeTracker3 will normally not allow removing recorded Events from the historical data. Again, someone acting as a "workspace administrator" may do anything they need within the managed workspace, including removal of old or irrelevant Events, but even then the practice is strongly discouraged.
