Workspaces

A TimeTracker3 Workspace is a container where daily activity data are recorded and can subsequently be observed, reported on, etc.

Why use workspaces

Using Workspaces (as opposed to e.g. single files where daily activity notes are taken) offers several advantages, such as:

  • Allowing organisations and teams to use a standardised solution for recording (and reporting on) their daily activities, instead of each worker doing that by their own means.
  • Imposing unified access control rules on the daily activities information.
  • Allowing easily integrating recorded daily activities data into combined reports.

Types of workspaces

Depending on the needs of a concrete project or organisation, a TimeTracker workspace can be:

  • A single file residing on a local or remote drive - this is a lightweight solution for small teams or individual developers.
  • A database residing on a local or shared server - this is an effective solution for larger organisations where multiple people from multiple departments all track their daily activities simultaneously.
  • A dedicated TimeTracker3 "storage server", which separates the worker's computers from the actual data storage, allowing the two to communicate securely over a WAN - this is best suited to large and internationally distributed organisations which make TimeTracker3 a business-wise working time recording solution.

Depending on the options chosen, the concrete TimeTracker3 installation may be configured with only these types of Workspaces which are actually needed.
